The next morning, when she woke up, both the prince and the castle were gone, and she was lying on a little green patch, in the midst of the thick, dark forest, and by her side lay the same bundle of rags she had brought with her from her old home.

When she had rubbed the sleep out of her eyes, and cried until she was tired, she set out on her way, and walked many, many days, until she came to a high cliff.

An old woman sat under it, and played with a golden apple which she tossed about. The girl asked “You shine into every crack, and over every peak. Have you not seen a little white dove flying?” her if she knew the way to the prince, who lived with his stepmother in the castle east of the sun and west of the moon, and who was to marry the princess with a nose three yards long.

“How did you come to know about him?” asked the old woman. “Maybe you are the girl who should have had him?”

Yes, she was.
